Three Adjustable Settings

Toneguard's innovative twist-adjust mechanism provides three distinct training modes, each designed to build specific aspects of your technique.

1

Pure Buzz Setting

Focus entirely on buzzing technique and sound. Build embouchure strength and control with isolated buzz training.

2

Blended Setting

Experience a perfect mix of buzzing and horn sound. This transitional setting helps transfer buzz technique to actual playing.

3

Normal Setting

Return to your instrument's full, rich tone while maintaining all the embouchure benefits developed through buzz training.

Meet Evan

I'm a French horn player with the Houston Youth Symphony with 4+ years of experience, and throughout my musical journey, I kept encountering the same frustrating pattern everywhere I went.

Every bass teacher, every masterclass, every method book emphasized buzzing as absolutely fundamental to brass playing, and they were right: buzzing develops correct embouchure, strengthens your "chops," and builds the foundation for great tone and technique.

But here's what never made sense to me: The teaching of the skill was completely disconnected. Students would buzz on the mouthpiece alone, then completely switch contexts to play the horn. For something so fundamental and important, this disconnect felt wrong.

As I progressed in my playing and experienced this gap firsthand in countless practice sessions, lessons, and symphonies, I realized I wasn't alone. This was a universal problem affecting brass players everywhere.

That's when I knew I had to create the solution. Toneguard bridges that gap, finally connecting buzzing practice directly to your instrument. It's the tool that allows you to build those fundamental skills in the most natural and effective way possible.

— Evan, Creator of Toneguard
French Horn Player, Houston Youth Symphony
